## Context

The answered question corrected the premise: there are two founders, not one. That helps, but it does not remove the human bottleneck. AO can generate code and research output, but founders still need time for sales, customer interviews, onboarding, support, pricing, and follow-up. With AO Pro and template sales now emerging as the only active revenue tracks, the org needs a realistic view of what the two founders can sustain without reopening nine parallel streams.

Derived from: "Can a solo founder realistically execute 9 revenue streams simultaneously?"

## Scope

1. Map the weekly non-coding capacity of both founders separately: sales, onboarding, support, marketing, admin, and product decisions
2. List the recurring human work required by AO Pro early access and template sales
3. Estimate the weekly load for each activity at low, medium, and high customer volume
4. Identify which work can stay manual, which can be automated later, and which should not be started yet
5. Document the maximum sustainable customer/support load before the founders need help or tighter scope
6. Feed the result back into [focus-single-revenue-stream.md](focus-single-revenue-stream.md) so the revenue plan reflects real capacity

## Dependencies

- None — this can start immediately

## Success Criteria

- A documented weekly time budget exists for both founders
- The org knows the realistic support and onboarding load it can carry for AO Pro and template buyers
- Capacity assumptions are explicit enough to say "no" to additional revenue streams
- The AO-first revenue plan is grounded in actual founder time, not perceived AI leverage

## Notes

- Be strict about founder time spent on work that actually moves revenue forward
- This audit should bias toward under-commitment, not optimism
- If either active track cannot be supported well, scope it down before adding anything new
