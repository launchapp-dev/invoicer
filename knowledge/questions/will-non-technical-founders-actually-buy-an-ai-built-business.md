## Context

The vision states: "Streamline the process for founders to launch businesses and for businesses to grow and manage themselves." The "grow autonomously" value proposition targets founders who want a business that "runs and improves itself" with AO agents handling maintenance. However, non-technical founders may not trust AI-generated codebases they don't understand. Technical founders may want control and not want "autonomous" changes. The positioning may appeal to neither segment.

## What We Know

- Target market: "founders" who want to launch and grow businesses
- Value prop: AI maintains the codebase so founder focuses on customers/growth
- Templates are "AO-built" and marketed as such ("180+ PRs in 7 days by AI")
- AO agents maintain codebases 24/7 autonomously
- Templates include complex tech: React Router 7, Hono, Drizzle, Better-Auth

## What We Don't Know

- Do non-technical founders want to buy a business that runs on code they can't understand?
- Do technical founders want "autonomous" AI changes to their codebase, or do they want assistance?
- Is "built by AI" a selling point or a liability for template buyers?
- What happens when AO's autonomous change breaks something and the founder can't debug it?
- Are we targeting the right persona, or should we focus on agencies/dev shops instead?
- What is the liability/liability-insurance implication of selling AI-maintained businesses?

## Suggested Investigation

1. **Persona validation**: Interview 5 non-technical founders and 5 technical founders about "AI-maintained business" concept
2. **Positioning test**: A/B test "AO-built" vs "production-ready" messaging on landing pages
3. **Support model design**: Define human escalation path when autonomous changes fail
4. **Template buyer research**: Survey people who bought SaaS templates (from any vendor) about their preferences
5. **Positioning pivot option**: Consider positioning AO as "dev team accelerator" instead of "autonomous business"
