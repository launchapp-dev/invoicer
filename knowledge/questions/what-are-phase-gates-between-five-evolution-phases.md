Vision.md defines a 5-phase product evolution: Foundation → Bootstrap Revenue → Platform → Cloud Platform → Ecosystem. The revenue roadmap defines a parallel 4-phase timeline (Weeks 1–4 → Months 2–4 → Months 4–8 → Months 8–18). Neither document defines what must be true to graduate from one phase to the next, what would trigger a pivot, or what happens if a phase fails to deliver its targets.

## What We Know

- Vision phases are qualitative: "prove AO can build codebases" (Phase 1), "sell templates" (Phase 2), "AO Cloud for teams" (Phase 3), etc.
- Revenue roadmap phases have time-based targets but no go/no-go criteria: Phase 1 targets $3K–$12K/month in weeks 1–4.
- The revenue projection range is extremely wide: $30K–$120K MRR at month 12 (4x spread).
- Phase 3–4 products (AO Cloud, Enterprise Licensing) require significant infrastructure investment that depends on Phase 1–2 generating cash.
- The risk table identifies "spread too thin across 12 products" as high likelihood, high impact — but the mitigation is "sequence strictly by priority" with no defined sequencing criteria.
- There is no documented kill criteria for any product line.

## What We Don't Know

- What revenue threshold triggers the move from Phase 2 to Phase 3? Is it $10K MRR? $30K? Breakeven?

3. **Model the downside**: Run the revenue projection at the low end only and check if the Phase 3 timeline is still feasible without external funding.
