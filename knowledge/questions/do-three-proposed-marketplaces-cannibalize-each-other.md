## Context

The ideas backlog proposes three separate marketplace products: LaunchApp Marketplace (#4, priority 8/10) for templates and plugins, AO Skill Marketplace (F4, priority 8/10) for AO skills, and AO Workflow Marketplace (F17, priority 8/10) for workflow packs. Additionally, the Claude Code Skill Studio (#15) implies a fourth marketplace surface. Each requires discovery UI, search, payment processing, creator tools, review systems, and version management. The revenue roadmap projects marketplace revenue at $10K-$50K/month but treats them as a single line item.

## What We Know

- Each marketplace requires: payment infrastructure, listing pages, search/discovery, creator onboarding, review/rating system, version management, and support for disputes/refunds.
- The org's plugin packs were bulk-scaffolded 2026-03-16/17 and likely contain empty scaffolds (per existing question). Marketplace success requires real, quality content.
- The revenue roadmap estimates 200-1,000 subscribers for the plugin marketplace by months 6-8. This requires a thriving creator ecosystem that doesn't exist yet.
- Successful marketplaces (Shopify App Store, VS Code Extensions, npm) typically require years and dedicated teams to reach critical mass.
- The SaaS template market itself only has a handful of proven revenue generators (ShipFast ~$500K, MakerKit, Supastarter).

## What We Don't Know

- Can a solo-founder org with an AI workforce operate even one marketplace, let alone three?
- Would a single unified marketplace (templates + skills + workflows) be more viable than three separate ones?
- What's the minimum viable seller count needed for each marketplace to provide real discovery value vs just being a static list?
- Are developers willing to pay for AO skills and workflows when the product itself has no established user base yet?

## Suggested Investigation

- Study the launch timeline of comparable developer marketplaces: How long did it take the Vercel template marketplace, Shopify App Store, or Raycast extensions to reach 50+ community-contributed items?
- Prototype a single unified marketplace at launchapp.dev that sells templates, skills, and workflows under one payment system — test whether the combined catalog feels richer than three empty ones.
- Determine the minimum viable content: would 10 quality items per category be enough to launch, or do marketplaces need 50+ to feel worthwhile?
- Check whether any AO CLI users have requested a skill/workflow marketplace, or if this is a supply-push idea.
