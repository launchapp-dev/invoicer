## Context

Round 3 introduces three enterprise-priced products: AO Compliance Engine ($199–$999/seat/month), MCP Gateway ($49–$199/month managed), and LaunchPad Auth Cloud ($19–$399/month). Combined, these represent $160K+/month in projected revenue at modest capture rates. But enterprise software requires capabilities the org has never demonstrated: sales cycles (3–12 months for compliance tools), SOC 2 / ISO 27001 certification (table stakes for regulated-industry buyers), SLA guarantees with financial penalties, customer success and onboarding, legal review of contracts and liability, and 24/7 support infrastructure.

The existing question about solo founder execution capacity focuses on bandwidth. This question focuses on a different gap: the organizational capabilities required to sell and support enterprise products, regardless of whether the code is built.

## What We Know

- The org is currently a solo founder operation with AI agent assistance
- Revenue projections show Compliance Engine targeting "regulated industries (fintech, healthtech, govtech)"
- These industries require vendor security assessments, compliance certifications, and contractual SLAs before procurement
- No existing product in the portfolio has been sold to an enterprise buyer
- The strategic question about legal/compliance readiness to accept payments remains open and unanswered
- Enterprise sales cycles for compliance tooling typically require 3–12 months of relationship building
- Competitors (Vanta, Drata, Secureframe) have raised $100M+ each and employ dedicated sales teams

## What We Don't Know

- Whether the org has any SOC 2, ISO 27001, or other compliance certification
- What the cost of achieving enterprise-sales readiness would be (certifications, legal, support)
- Whether enterprise pricing is viable without enterprise infrastructure, or if this is aspirational pricing
- If there's a path to enterprise revenue that doesn't require a full enterprise sales motion (e.g., product-led growth with self-serve)
- Whether the Compliance Engine's target customers would trust an AI-generated compliance tool from a solo-founder startup

## Suggested Investigation

- Research minimum viable enterprise readiness: what certifications, legal structures, and support SLAs do fintech/healthtech buyers actually require?
- Price out SOC 2 Type II certification (typically $50K–$150K and 6–12 months)
- Evaluate whether enterprise products should be repositioned as self-serve developer tools at lower price points
- Study how single-founder companies like Plausible Analytics and Cal.com approached enterprise sales
- Determine if a channel partner strategy (resell through established compliance vendors) could bypass the need for direct enterprise sales
