## Context

The updated vision (vision.md) makes a bold positioning choice: templates are framed as "test runs and demonstrations of what AO can do." The marketing narrative is explicitly "this entire app was built by an AI workforce." This is simultaneously the core AO sales pitch and the template sales pitch — but these two audiences may have opposing reactions to the same message.

## What We Know

- Vision.md states: "The SaaS templates, the design system, the plugin packs — these are not standalone products. They are test runs and demonstrations of what AO can do."
- Template pricing is $149–$299 per vertical, with projected revenue of $5K–$20K/month at month 12.
- Competitors (ShipFast, Supastarter) market on human expertise, proven architecture, and founder credibility — not AI authorship.
- The AO buyer persona (developer who wants AI orchestration) is different from the template buyer persona (founder who wants a production-ready starter to ship fast).
- AI-generated code quality concerns are already flagged as a risk in existing questions (are-we-quality-gating-ai-generated-code-at-current-volume.md).

## What We Don't Know

- Do template buyers perceive "built by AI" as a quality signal (cutting-edge, well-tested) or a risk signal (experimental, untested, no human judgment)?
- Would framing templates as "maintained by AO" (implying continuous improvement) land differently than "built by AO" (implying no human involvement)?
- Are there segments of the template market that would pay MORE for AI-built templates (e.g., AI-forward teams) vs. segments that would pay LESS?
- Does calling templates "test cases" undermine the buyer's confidence that the product was built for THEIR needs vs. as an internal demo?

## Suggested Investigation

1. **Customer interviews**: Talk to 5–10 potential template buyers and A/B test the pitch — one version emphasizes "built by experienced developers," the other emphasizes "built and maintained by AI workforce."
2. **Competitor analysis**: Check if any SaaS template competitors have tried "AI-built" positioning and what happened.
3. **Landing page test**: Create two versions of the template sales page with different framing and measure conversion.
4. **Community sentiment**: Search Reddit, Indie Hackers, and Twitter for reactions to "AI-generated code" in production contexts — is the sentiment positive or negative in the target demographic?
